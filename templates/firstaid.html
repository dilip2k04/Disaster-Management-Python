First Aid & CPR Instructions

Clear, step-by-step emergency procedures that can help save lives in critical situations

How to Perform CPR (Adults)

1

Check for Responsiveness

Tap the person's shoulder firmly and shout "Are you okay?" Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing (no more than 10 seconds).

2

Call Emergency Services

If no response and no normal breathing, immediately call 112 (India) or ask someone nearby to call while you begin compressions.

3

Start Chest Compressions

Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest (lower half of sternum). Place other hand on top, interlock fingers. Push hard and fast — about 5–6 cm deep at 100–120 compressions per minute.

4

Give Rescue Breaths (if trained)

After 30 compressions, open airway (tilt head back, lift chin), pinch nose, and give 2 breaths (each lasting about 1 second — watch chest rise).

5

Continue CPR Cycles

Keep repeating 30 compressions + 2 breaths until help arrives, the person shows signs of life, or you are too exhausted to continue.

Important: If you're not trained in rescue breaths, perform compression-only CPR (hands-only). It's still very effective.

First Aid for Choking (Conscious Adult)

1

Recognize Choking

Person cannot speak, cough forcefully, or breathe. They may clutch their throat (universal choking sign).

2

Ask "Are you choking?"

If they nod or cannot answer — act immediately.

3

Perform Abdominal Thrusts (Heimlich Maneuver)

Stand behind them. Wrap arms around waist, make a fist above navel, grasp with other hand, and give quick inward & upward thrusts.

4

Continue until object is dislodged

Repeat thrusts until the blockage is cleared or the person becomes unconscious. If unconscious → start CPR.

Control of Severe Bleeding

1

Apply Direct Pressure

Use a clean cloth, bandage, or gloved hand to press firmly on the wound.

2

Elevate if possible

Raise the injured limb above heart level (unless fracture is suspected).

3

Add more dressings if soaked

Do not remove original dressing — add more layers on top and keep pressure.

4

Call 112 immediately

Severe bleeding is life-threatening. Get emergency help as soon as possible.
